2. Personal Data

2.1. Data Collected and Purpose

When registering for the hospital service application, some data is collected for specific purposes. For authentication, identification and creation of user accounts, we collect email, phone number, password, license number and role. To guarantee continuity of care and personalized treatments, we require medical records, which includes allergies and medical conditions, appointment details, date of birth, gender, emergency contact, medical record number and address. Additionally, for communication to inform about any changes regarding the user information, it is necessary email, phone number, address and name.

All data collected is processed and stored in accordance with the principles of the GDPR, ensuring transparency and the security and integrity of the system’s activities. We also emphasize that the data is collected for specific purposes and in accordance with the legal provision outlined in Article 5(1)(c) of the GDPR.

2.2. Retention of Personal Data

Personal data will be retained for as long as the user remains active within the hospital application, as the data provided is essential for delivering optimal and the most comprehensive service to users. When a user requests the deletion of their account, the account data will be removed after a 30-day period, after which it will no longer be accessible and will be permanently removed from the system.

On the other hand, medical records will be stored in accordance with the guidelines set forth in the option of the ERS (Health Insurance Entity), and the retention period will be of 5 years.

2.3. Lawful Bases for Processing Personal Data

The hospital processes personal data based on the following legal grounds:

  • Vital Interest (Article 6(1)(d)): when processing is essential to safeguard the life or physical integrity of the data subject or another individual, especially in cases where the data subject cannot provide consent due to physical or legal incapacity.
  • Legitimate Interest (Article 6(1)(f)): to ensure the provision of efficient and secure application services critical to operational effectiveness.
  • Consent (Articles 6(1)(a) and 9(2)(a)): when the data subject has explicitly provided informed consent that the data concerning them will be processed.
  • Public Health (Article 9(2)(i)): to address health threats or to maintain the standards of healthcare, medicinal products, or medical device safety and quality.
  • Data Subject (Article 9(2)(c)): when necessary to protect the life or well-being of the data subject or another individual, particularly in situations where consent cannot be obtained.
  • Courts (Article 9(2)(f)): when processing is necessary for the establishment, exercise or defence of legal claims, or when courts are acting in their judicial capacity.

3. Rights

3.1. User Rights

Being a data subject in our system, said data subject is given several rights under GDPR regulation, such as:

  • Right of Information – Article 13: when personal data is collected from the data subject, all necessary information will be provided at the time of collection.
  • Right of Access – Article 15: the data subject has the right to obtain confirmation as to whether personal data concerning them are being processed, and access to the personal data and all the relevant information.
  • Right of Rectification – Article 16: the data subject has the right to the rectification of inaccurate personal data concerning him or her.
  • Right to Erasure – Article 17: the data subject has the right to erasure the personal data concerning him or her without undue delay.
  • Right of Data Portability – Article 20: the data subject has the right to receive the personal data concerning him or her in a structured, commonly used format.
  • Right to Object – Article 21: the data subject has the right to object, on grounds relating to their situation, at any time to processing of personal data concerning them.

3.2. Complaints

If you believe your personal data has been processed in violation of the GDPR, you can file a complaint with the CNPD (Comissão Nacional de Proteção de Dados).
